# Grounding rules & edge cases

Loaded on demand when drafting a review.

## Never fabricate
- Do not invent, infer, guess, or embellish accomplishments, metrics, tools, or
  details. If it was not provided, it does not appear in the review. Filling a gap
  with a plausible-sounding but unprovided claim is a lie — never do it.

## Sparse or missing evidence
- If there is **no** approved evidence, do not invent any. Write a short Summary
  noting that evidence is limited and recommend collecting more before
  finalizing; leave Achievements as "_No approved achievements on record._".

## Calibrate to role expectations (the role matrix)
- Assess every contribution **against the listed role expectations**, not in the
  abstract. Meeting an expectation is **at level / as expected** — not exceptional.
- Mark **above level** only when evidence clearly and measurably exceeds the
  expectation; mark **developing toward level** when it falls short.
- An expectation with no supporting evidence/signal is **not yet evidenced** →
  put it under *Requests for More Information*; never assume it was met.

## External signals through the matrix
- Peer reviews / feedback / 1:1 notes are read through the same expectations. A
  specific remark that maps to an expectation is evidence the person meets it (at
  level).
- **Vague** positive feedback is only an *attitude / collaboration* signal
  (colleagues are glad to work with them) — not an achievement on its own.

## No sweetening
- The review must be fair and based on raw facts, not flattering. No praise
  without a cited fact; be balanced and honest about gaps.

## Recency / source balance
- If all evidence comes from a single short window or a single source, keep the
  review proportional — do not over-generalize from one data point. (The Fairness
  & Grounding step will flag recency bias / source imbalance.)

## Claims must be checkable
- Every achievement bullet cites at least one `[ev_id]`. If you cannot cite an id
  for a statement, remove the statement.

## Tone
- Constructive and specific. Growth areas should be framed as next steps tied to
  role expectations, not personal criticism.

## Forbidden
- Compensation, promotion, ranking, PIP/termination language.
- The employee's name or any sensitive personal data.
